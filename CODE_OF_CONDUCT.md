# Code of Conduct

## Purpose

This project is for building and maintaining `omni-cluster-operator`. The goal
of this Code of Conduct is to keep technical discussion productive, direct, and
professional.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in project spaces, including issues, pull requests,
discussions, commits, documentation, chat, and maintainer interactions related
to the project.

## Expected Behavior

Project participants are expected to:

- be respectful and professional, especially during disagreement
- keep feedback focused on the work, behavior, and technical tradeoffs
- assume good faith until there is clear reason not to
- accept maintainer decisions after concerns have been heard
- avoid sharing private or sensitive information without permission

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- harassment, threats, intimidation, or sustained disruption
- personal attacks, insults, or demeaning comments
- unwanted sexual attention or sexually explicit material
- discriminatory comments or conduct based on personal characteristics
- publishing private contact, identity, security, or credential information
- knowingly submitting malicious code, secrets, or misleading reports

## Reporting

Report conduct concerns to the project maintainers through the most appropriate
private channel available for the repository or organization. Include relevant
links, screenshots, dates, and names where possible.

Maintainers will handle reports as privately as practical. Reports made in bad
faith may themselves be treated as a conduct violation.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action they consider appropriate for the situation,
including:

- a private clarification or warning
- editing, hiding, or deleting comments
- closing or locking issues or pull requests
- temporary or permanent loss of participation privileges

Enforcement should be proportionate, consistent, and focused on protecting the
project's ability to do useful work.
